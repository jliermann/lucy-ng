<objective>
Add a deterministic `multiplicity_edited` boolean (plus `negative_crosspeak_count`) to `lucy pick hsqc --format json`, ported verbatim from the existing `lucy pick 1d` `negative_detected` detector. This is the single unit-testable Python seam of Phase 88 and the programmatic basis for D-05 / MULT-04: it lets the nmr-chemist decide "is this HSQC multiplicity-edited?" by reading a boolean instead of eyeballing the spectrum.

Purpose: Removes human judgment from the HSQC half of the ambiguity trigger (the CASE4 failure where a non-edited HSQC was treated as multiplicity-determinate). A multiplicity-edited HSQC has phased CH2 cross-peaks of opposite sign → genuine negative intensity well below the noise floor; no negatives ⇒ NOT edited ⇒ sign-ambiguous.

Output: `pick.py` enhancement + true/false unit tests in `tests/test_cli_pick.py` (with a synthesized negative-crosspeak fixture in `conftest.py`).
</objective>
