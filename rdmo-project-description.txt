lucy-ng — AI-Agent-Powered Computer-Assisted Structure Elucidation (CASE) for Natural Products

Scientific scope
lucy-ng addresses the long-standing problem of determining the chemical structure of unknown
organic natural products from nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) spectra. Natural products remain
a cornerstone of drug discovery, chemical ecology, and metabolomics, but elucidating their
structures from spectroscopic evidence is time-consuming, expertise-dependent, and error-prone.
lucy-ng automates this workflow by coupling large-language-model agents (Claude) with the
established logic-based structure generator LSD (Nuzillard, Univ. Reims) and a curated reference
database of known natural products. The agents translate raw NMR evidence into formal structural
constraints, run the LSD solver, rank candidate structures by predicted versus observed 13C
chemical shifts, and iteratively refine the constraint set — replicating the reasoning a trained
NMR chemist would apply. The package supports two complementary modes: dereplication (matching
an unknown against ~928,000 known compounds) and de novo structure elucidation when no database
match exists.

Input data
Primary inputs are Bruker-format NMR datasets for a single compound at natural abundance,
typically comprising a 1H 1D, a 13C 1D, DEPT-135 (and optionally DEPT-90), edited HSQC, HMBC,
and COSY experiments, together with the molecular formula obtained from high-resolution mass
spectrometry. Dataset sizes range from a few MB to ~100 MB per compound.

Reference data
A single SQLite database (~2.8 GB uncompressed, ~830 MB compressed, DOI 10.6084/m9.figshare.31073554)
integrates 928,443 compounds from COCONUT and NMRShiftDB with 13C chemical shift assignments and
7.9 million precomputed HOSE-code shift statistics used for structure ranking.

Generated data
For each CASE run, lucy-ng produces machine-readable peak lists, statistically derived structural
constraints (hybridisation, neighbour atoms, hetero-hetero bonds, symmetry groups), LSD input and
solution files, deduplicated candidate structures in SMILES/InChI format with provenance metadata,
a ranked shortlist scored by mean absolute error against predicted 13C shifts, and a full
human-readable progress log (CASE-PROGRESS.md) documenting every reasoning step, intervention,
and solver iteration for full reproducibility and auditability.
